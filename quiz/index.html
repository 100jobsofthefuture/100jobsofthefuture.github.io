100 Jobs of the Future

What might future work look like for you?

In the future, could you be a smart dust wrangler? A biofilm plumber? A robot ethicist? An offworld habitat designer? A personal brand manager? Read more

Everyone has characteristics that make them unique. Different people have different combinations of interests, aptitudes, and skills.

This Jobs explorer tool has been designed to identify some of your career interests and aptitudes. It is not a fully detailed assessment — while you may have stronger interests in some areas than in others, you may also find that you can relate to more than one.

On the basis of your answers, the jobs explorer will recommend some of the ‘100 Jobs of the Future’ that you might like to explore.

Here is a list of areas of future work.

Select 2 to 3 to explore examples of future jobs in those areas

  • Localisation is a trend that is about reconnecting people with their local places, cultures and communities. It is a counter movement to globalisation and the development of big cities.
  • As more and more data can be collected about people’s specific interests and needs, it becomes possible to create personalised products, services and experiences for them.
  • Advances in digital technologies and food science mean that highly nutritious food can be produced efficiently and with minimum environmental impact.
  • The future of leisure and entertainment is immersive, digital, and personalised.
  • With advances in healthcare and nutrition, we will live longer, and have more opportunities to enjoy life.
  • Constant change means that people will also constantly be watching the future, retraining and upskilling.
  • With advances in artificial intelligence and other digital technologies, we need to have new regulations and laws to make sure everyone is safe.
  • New digital technologies and advances in science mean that there will be many new ways to enhance our health and wellbeing.
  • Key areas of predicted future scientific discovery include the microbiome (microbes living inside us), and human and food genetics.
  • Technological advancements will help us ensure environmental sustainability and restore the natural environment.
  • Advanced city design will improve liveability and environmental sustainability of our urban areas.
  • Advances in digital technology and use of big data mean constant new ideas and innovations. People will drive many of these innovations and new business ideas, and other people will regulate and make sure that the ideas are lawful and ethical.
  • Our activities in space will expand, from space tourism to advanced satellite technologies and offworld living.
  • Much of the future of science and technology is very small indeed. Nanotechnology happens at the level of atoms and molecules and has applications across health, the environment, the design of cities, and what we eat.
  • Our lives will be enhanced by the digital world superimposed onto the ‘real world’ such as through visual overlays and immersive digital experiences.
  • Advanced robots will help us in every aspect of our lives, from childcare to self-driving vehicles to drone-based shopping delivery.
  • Artificial intelligence technologies learn, react and behave like humans. AI will be useful to help us make sense of our data and make complex decisions.
  • Thanks to the Internet of Things, in the future sensors will collect data about every aspect of our lives, from our health and eating habits to levels of pollution in the environment. The Sensors are networked, and the data is used to provide advice and make decisions.
  • In the future, enormous printers will be able to print entire buildings or vehicles, while advances in materials science mean items can be made of new materials with different properties such as greater strength and flexibility.
